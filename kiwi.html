Kiwi Case Study

Health + Wellness Responsive Web App





ROLES:

Business Requirements Document; User Research; Competitive Analysis; Surveys and Interviews; User Personas; User Stories; Sitemap; Affinity Map; User Journey; User Flows; Low- Mid- High-Fidelity Prototypes and Wireframes; Usability Test Plan, Script, Testing; A/B Preference Testing; Qualitative and Quantitative Report; Design Language System; Style Guide

TOOLS:

Adobe XD; Photoshop CC; Optimal Card Sort; Google Survey



PROBLEM STATEMENT:

Society needs a way to educate themselves on proper nutrition and have tools to help them consume a healthy diet because the impact of improper nutrition affects the overall wellbeing of mental and physical health.

HYPOTHESIS STATEMENT:

We will know this to be true when we see an increase in positive mental health and life fulfillment, a decline in obesity, and proper nutrition in children and adults through in-app user data calculated by user specific achievements and sliding scales.

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY:

Our health and well-being guide will be a responsive web app that features nutritional information, healthy recipes, a meal planner, and a user profile to track their progress, build a favorite recipes list, and bookmark nutritional information. Our platform is for users of any age who are wanting to educate themselves and improve their mental and physical health through proper nutrition.

Our target audience are women ages 25-45 because the metabolism starts to slow down, hormonal imbalances from pregnancy occur, they tend to suffer from body image issues, and lifestyle priorities are packed to the brim which leads to buying for convenience. Statistically speaking, women still perform a majority of the household duties, like grocery shopping and cooking, and our product will be a way to properly nourish the whole family.

RISKS + OPPORTUNITY:

The biggest risk is not standing out in an oversaturated market. Competing with platforms that have been featured on big named brands like Better Homes + Gardens and Women’s Daily, as well as, having a strong social media following.

The number one opportunity is offering straight-forward information for users who have busy lives and don’t have time to do extensive research or reading on proper nutrition and offering healthy recipes ready to add to a meal plan. Our web app will fill a gap between too little information with tools and too much information without tools, providing just the right amount for successful lifestyle changes when it comes to proper nutrition.

SURVEYS + INTERVIEWS:

My three interviewees all have children ranging in ages newborn to seven, are all in their early 30’s, all have husbands, and all live completely different lifestyles. Their occupation strongly influences their food choices during the day but they all eat a well-balanced dinner when they actually cook. In the survey, I found that people without kids cook less and people with kids cook more and those options can be based on what the kids will actually eat. Holding themselves accountable and visually seeing their progress makes them feel like they’re actually making better choices. Every participant in the survey and interviews said they were either too tired or just didn’t have the energy at the end of the day to cook. I got fantastic feedback from both the survey and the interviews and I got extensive information for all three of my research goals.

"Physically I feel guilty, horrible, worthless, fat, bloated, like garbage. During my Weight Watchers days I felt great, airy, beautiful, free, capable of things."

2 out of 3 of them said their doctors didn’t provide them with any further information when their bloodwork was out of healthy ranges.

"100% what I consume makes me not function at my full potential but also mental health plays a role. When I was doing Weight Watchers, I felt great all the time."

"I’ve gotten better about planning ahead due to budgeting, a free for all at the store isn’t working."

AFFINITY MAP






FUTURE FEATURES


I will emphasize on the build of the progress tracker. This feature was not part of the three core functions of the assignment and as my design progressed, I realized it was crucial to success, for the user and the design. The original hypothesis statement would then be validated by learning how people judge their levels of success. What levels of accountability do people need to be held to? What types of visuals do people respond to, like sliding scales or closed rings? Do people need predetermined choices to choose from or enter their own or both?

Along with the progress tracker, the populated grocery list is another specific improvement as this sets my project apart from most competitors. To keep the concept simple, a user would select specific days and a grocery list would populate based on the meals planned. The user would be able to check off ingredients as they’re shopping and delete them if they already have them at home. There would also be a “staples list” function as most households have go to products.

A third improvement would be to the specific day screen in the meal planner. This could feature a circle graph with the DV% based on chosen meals, calorie counts, portion sizes, and nutritional information. This would help keep users on track to not exceed daily values.
